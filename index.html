Orientation

BOBCAT is a simple site that catalogs other sites that look good when rendered as text with the Lynx Web Browser.

Below is a list of web site which are usable by Lynx users. Simply scroll down until you see something interesting and either press <enter> to visit the site or press 'a', then 'l' to save the link to your bookmarks.

We also have a FAQ.

The Catalog

Duck Duck Go Search
The good people at DuckDuckGo maintain a low complexity site that's easy to use and has zero cruft.
Text-Only NPR.org
NPR maintains a "thin" version of their site which works well with text-based browsers.
CNN Headlines
This is the "lite" version of the CNN web page. I bet it was created for older mobile devices, but it renders beautifully with Lynx and doesn't bombard you with ads.
68k.news : Headlines from the Future
68k.news maintains a text-friendly mirror into the US edition of the Google News. The cert on the site is borked, but that ties in with the retrocomputing theme.
Hacker News
If you're unfamiliar with Hacker News, it's rather like the SlashDot of old, but with a greatly simplified landing page and slightly less drama.
Lobsters
"Lobsters is a computing-focused community centered around link aggregation and discussion." It's also the home of a pleasant and informed community.
The Digital Services Playbook
From the U.S. Digital Services, the playbook lists thirteen key "plays" describing successful "Best Common Practices."
World Wide Web (The Project)
This is the world's first web page, archived at CERN and is delightfully spartan.
My Home Page
Of course my home page is going to be pleasant to look at with lynx. I have some technical writings and fiction I wrote. It's fun, if you're into that kind of thing.
